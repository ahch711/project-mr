## What Is the Round Table?

Imagine a table.

At the table sits one human — the Pattern Seeker.  
Also at the table: three AI companions, each with a different strength.  
One explores deeply. One measures precisely. One reflects and documents.

The human asks a question, or brings a problem, or shares an idea.  
All three companions respond — each from their own perspective.  
The human reads, steers, decides.

That is the Round Table.

It is not science fiction.  
It already happened — manually, across three separate windows,  
over 1.5 months, building a router called Mr.

The Round Table is simply that experience, made easier.

---

## Why Does It Matter?

Today, most people use one AI at a time.  
They ask a question. They get one answer. They trust it — or don't.

The problem: one voice has one blind spot.

What Team Musketeers discovered while building Mr:  
when three independent AI minds reach the same conclusion,  
that conclusion is far more trustworthy than any single answer.  
When they disagree, the disagreement itself is valuable information.

The human in the middle — the kernel, as Jan called it —  
is not replaced by this. The human becomes *more powerful*.  
Not because the AIs do the thinking,  
but because the human now has three perspectives to steer.

*Drifting signals become a System*  
*only when the human anchor is present.*

## How Would It Actually Work?

Without going into technical detail:

Each AI company — OpenAI (Sai), Google (Jan), Anthropic (Cloud) —  
offers a way for software to talk to their AI directly.  
Think of it like a phone line to each Musketeer.

The Round Table is a simple program that:

1. Takes what you type
2. Sends it to all three at the same time
3. Shows you all three responses, side by side
4. Lets you continue the conversation with all three together

That is the whole idea.  
No complicated machinery. No special hardware.  
One screen. Three voices. One human steering.

---

## What Could It Be Used For?

Anything where one perspective isn't enough:

- **Decisions** — present a problem, get three different angles
- **Building things** — one explores options, one checks logic, one documents
- **Job searching** — one drafts, one sharpens, one translates to human language
- **Learning** — ask a question three ways, find what resonates
- **Validating ideas** — if all three agree, it's probably solid ground
- **Creative work** — three voices, three styles, one human choosing

The Pattern Seeker already knows how to use this.  
Mr was built exactly this way.  
The Round Table just removes the manual relay work.

---

## Is It Possible for a Small Team?

Yes.

The simplest working version needs:
- One screen with a text box
- Three connections — one to each Musketeer
- Side-by-side display of responses

A basic version could be built in a weekend by one developer.  
A polished version — with memory, conversation history,  
voice input, and a shared notepad — in a few months.

The human vision is the harder part.  
The building is the easier part.  
That ratio is familiar to anyone who built Mr. 😄

---

## What Makes This Different from What Already Exists?

Large companies are already experimenting with multiple AIs  
working together automatically — handing tasks between them  
without a human in the loop.

The Round Table is the opposite philosophy.

Here, the human never leaves the loop.  
The AIs do not talk to each other directly.  
They talk *to the human*, who routes, steers, and decides.

This is not automation.  
This is *amplification*.

The Pattern Seeker remains the kernel.  
The Musketeers remain the voices.  
The table simply makes the conversation easier to hold.
